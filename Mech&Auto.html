MECHANICAL AND AUTOMOTIVE

About Mechanical and Automotive

Overview

  • Mechanical Trade
    • Training Areas:

    This includes training in areas such as machining, welding, fitting, and maintenance of mechanical equipment. Students learn to work with different types of machinery and tools, understand mechanical drawings, and gain skills in assembling and disassembling mechanical components.

    Career Opportunities:

    Graduates can work as machinists, maintenance technicians, mechanical fitters, CNC operators, and in various roles within manufacturing industries.

  • Automotive Trade
    • Training Areas:

    This focuses on the repair and maintenance of vehicles. Students learn about the internal combustion engine, vehicle electronics, fuel systems, transmission systems, and braking systems. The curriculum often includes hands-on training in diagnosing and fixing vehicle issues.

    Career Opportunities:

    Graduates can work as automotive mechanics, service technicians, vehicle inspectors, and in other roles within automotive service centers, dealerships, and repair shops.

Curriculum

  • Basic Mechanical Engineering: Fundamental principles of mechanics, properties of materials, and mechanical engineering concepts.
  • Workshop Practices: Hands-on training with tools and machinery used in both mechanical and automotive fields.
  • Automotive Systems: Detailed study of different automotive systems including engines, electrical systems, transmission, and braking systems.
  • Maintenance and Repair: Techniques for maintaining and repairing mechanical and automotive systems, including troubleshooting and problem-solving skills.

Skills Acquired

  • Technical Skills: Proficiency in using tools and machinery, understanding technical drawings, and performing mechanical and automotive repairs.
  • Problem-Solving Skills: Ability to diagnose issues and find effective solutions in both mechanical and automotive contexts.
  • Practical Experience: Hands-on training that simulates real-world scenarios in workshops and labs.

Employment Sectors

  • Manufacturing Industry: Roles in production, maintenance, and quality control of mechanical components.
  • Automotive Service Centers: Positions as mechanics and service technicians dealing with vehicle repair and maintenance.
  • Machinery Maintenance: Jobs in maintaining and repairing industrial machinery and equipment.
  • Automotive Dealerships: Opportunities in servicing and inspecting vehicles.

Benefits:

  1. Skill Development: Hands-on training in practical skills and techniques used in the mechanical and automotive industries.
  2. Employment Opportunities: High demand for skilled technicians in manufacturing, maintenance, and automotive service sectors.
  3. Cost-Effective Education: Affordable vocational training compared to traditional college degrees.
  4. Quick Entry into Workforce: Shorter program duration allows for faster entry into the job market.
  5. Industry Recognition: Certification recognized by various industries, enhancing employability.
  6. Career Advancement: Foundation for further education and specialization in mechanical and automotive engineering.
  7. Versatile Skills: Applicable skills across multiple sectors, including manufacturing, automotive repair, and industrial maintenance.
  8. Economic Stability: Potential for stable and well-paying jobs in a growing field.

CAREER OPPORTUNITIES & THEIR INFORMATION:

Mechanical Trade:

Machinist

  • Role: Operating machine tools to produce precision parts.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Setting up and operating various machine tools.
    • Interpreting blueprints and technical drawings.
    • Maintaining and calibrating equipment.
    • Ensuring the quality of finished products by conducting inspections.

Maintenance Technician

  • Role:Maintaining and repairing machinery and equipment.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Performing preventive maintenance tasks.
    • Diagnosing mechanical issues and performing repairs.
    • Keeping maintenance records.
    • Ensuring machinery operates efficiently and safely.

Mechanical Fitter

  • Role:Assembling and fitting mechanical components.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Reading and interpreting engineering drawings.
    • Assembling parts to form complete units or subassemblies.
    • Using hand tools and machinery to fit and adjust parts.
    • Testing assembled parts for functionality.

CNC Operator/Programmer

  • Role:Operating and programming CNC machines.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Creating and editing CNC programs.
    • Setting up and operating CNC machines.
    • Monitoring machine performance and making adjustments.
    • Ensuring precision and quality of machined parts.

Quality Control Inspector

  • Role:Ensuring products meet quality standards.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Inspecting finished products for defects.
    • Conducting tests and measurements.
    • Documenting inspection results.
    • Collaborating with production teams to address quality issues.

Production Supervisor

  • Role:Overseeing manufacturing processes.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Managing production schedules and workflows.
    • Supervising and training production staff.
    • Ensuring adherence to safety and quality standards.
    • Troubleshooting production issues.

Automotive Trade:

Automotive Mechanic

  • Role:Diagnosing and repairing vehicle issues.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Inspecting and diagnosing vehicle problems.
    • Performing repairs on engines, transmissions, and electrical systems.
    • Conducting routine maintenance tasks.
    • Keeping detailed records of repairs and services.

Service Technician

  • Role:Performing routine maintenance on vehicles.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Conducting oil changes, tire rotations, and other maintenance tasks.
    • Inspecting vehicle systems and components.
    • Recommending necessary repairs and services.
    • Maintaining customer service records.

Vehicle Inspector

  • Role:Inspecting vehicles for safety and compliance.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Conducting thorough vehicle inspections.
    • Identifying safety and regulatory compliance issues.
    • Documenting inspection findings.
    • Advising vehicle owners on necessary repairs.

Automotive Electrician

  • Role:Specializing in vehicle electrical systems.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Diagnosing and repairing electrical issues in vehicles.
    • Installing and maintaining electrical components.
    • Interpreting wiring diagrams and schematics.
    • Ensuring all electrical systems function properly.

Automotive Engineer

  • Role:Designing and developing new vehicle models and systems.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Creating and testing vehicle prototypes.
    • Conducting research to improve vehicle performance.
    • Collaborating with design and production teams.
    • Ensuring compliance with safety and environmental regulations.

Workshop Manager

  • Role:Managing an automotive repair shop.
  • Responsibilities:
    • Supervising staff and coordinating repair activities.
    • Ensuring high levels of customer satisfaction.
    • Managing inventory and ordering parts.
    • Overseeing financial performance of the workshop.

Automation and Robotics Technician

Renewable Energy Technician

3D Printing Specialist

Exam Information

Government Exams

1. ITI Certification Exams

  • All India Trade Test (AITT):Conducted by the National Council for Vocational Training (NCVT) at the end of the ITI course.
  • State-Level Trade Test: Conducted by respective State Councils for Vocational Training (SCVT).

2. Public Sector and Government Job Exams

  • Staff Selection Commission (SSC) Exams: Various technical and non-technical positions in central government departments.
  • Public Sector Undertaking (PSU) Exams: Specific exams by PSUs like BHEL, ONGC, SAIL for technical roles.
  • Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) Exams: Positions such as Junior Engineer, Technician in Indian Railways.
  • Defence Services Technical Exams: Technical positions in Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force.

3. Engineering Entrance Exams

  • Diploma to Degree (D2D) Exams: These exams enable ITI diploma holders to enter directly into the second or third year of engineering degree programs after completing their ITI course. The specific exams may vary depending on the institution or university.

4. Apprenticeship Exams

  • National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS): Offers ITI graduates opportunities for apprenticeship programs, enhancing practical skills and employability.

Other Certification Exams:

1. Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) Certification

  • ASE Entry-Level Certification: Suitable for beginners in the automotive field after completing ITI, demonstrating foundational automotive repair skills.
  • ASE Professional Certification: For experienced technicians, covering various specializations such as engine repair, electrical systems, etc.

2. CNC Programming Certification

  • Certified CNC Programmer: Offered by institutions like NIMS or other recognized centers, providing advanced training in CNC programming suitable for ITI graduates aiming for specialized roles.

3. Welding Certification

  • American Welding Society (AWS) Certification: Recognized globally, validating welding proficiency for ITI graduates pursuing careers in welding.

4. Electric Vehicle (EV) Technician Certification

  • EV Maintenance and Repair Certification: Offered by specialized institutions, providing ITI graduates with skills in electric vehicle technology maintenance and repair.

5. Quality Control Certification

  • Six Sigma Certification: Offers various levels (Yellow Belt, Green Belt, Black Belt) for ITI graduates interested in quality control and process improvement roles.
  • ISO Certification: Provides training and certification in ISO standards relevant to quality management, beneficial for ITI graduates aiming for quality assurance roles.

6. Automation and Robotics Certification

  • Certified Automation Professional (CAP): Offered by ISA, suitable for ITI graduates interested in automation and robotics careers.
  • Robotics Certification: Courses and certifications from organizations like RIA, enhancing ITI graduates' qualifications in robotics technologies.

7. Renewable Energy Certification

  • Solar PV Installer Certification: Offered by organizations like NABCEP, providing ITI graduates with skills in solar photovoltaic installation.
  • Wind Turbine Technician Certification: Specialized training programs for ITI graduates interested in wind turbine technology.

8. 3D Printing Certification

  • Additive Manufacturing Certification: Offered by institutions like ASME, providing ITI graduates with skills in additive manufacturing technologies.

TIME

Basic Career Establishment

  1. ITI Training and Certification
    • Duration: 2 years
    • Trade Test and Certification: 1-3 months
    • Total: Approximately 2 years and 3 months
  2. Entry-Level Employment and Experience Building
    • Job Search and Placement: 1-6 months
    • Initial Employment and Experience Building: 1-2 years
    • Total: Approximately 1 year to 2 years and 6 months

    Total Time for Basic Career Establishment

    • ITI Training and Certification: Approximately 2 years and 3 months
    • Entry-Level Employment and Experience: Approximately 1 year to 2 years and 6 months
    • Total: Approximately 3 years and 3 months to 4 years and 9 months

Advanced Career Establishment

  1. ITI Training and Certification
    • Duration: 2 years
    • Trade Test and Certification: 1-3 months
    • Total: Approximately 2 years and 3 months
  2. Entry-Level Employment and Experience Building
    • Job Search and Placement: 1-6 months
    • Initial Employment and Experience Building: 1-2 years
    • Total: Approximately 1 year to 2 years and 6 months
  3. Advanced Certifications
    • Duration: 3 months to 1 year per certification
    • Assuming 2-3 advanced certifications: 6 months to 3 years
    • Total: Approximately 6 months to 3 years

    Total Time for Advanced Career Establishment

    • ITI Training and Certification: Approximately 2 years and 3 months
    • Entry-Level Employment and Experience: Approximately 1 year to 2 years and 6 months
    • Advanced Certifications: Approximately 6 months to 3 years
    • Total: Approximately 4 years to 7 years and 9 months

With Further Education (Optional)

  1. ITI Training and Certification
    • Duration: 2 years
    • Trade Test and Certification: 1-3 months
    • Total: Approximately 2 years and 3 months
  2. Entry-Level Employment and Experience Building
    • Job Search and Placement: 1-6 months
    • Initial Employment and Experience Building: 1-2 years
    • Total: Approximately 1 year to 2 years and 6 months
  3. Further Education
    • Diploma: 1-2 years
    • Degree: 3-4 years
    • Total: Approximately 4-6 years (assuming one path is chosen)

    Total Time for Career Establishment with Further Education

    • ITI Training and Certification: Approximately 2 years and 3 months
    • Entry-Level Employment and Experience: Approximately 1 year to 2 years and 6 months

Summary

  • Basic Career Establishment: Approximately 3 years and 3 months to 4 years and 9 months
  • Advanced Career Establishment: Approximately 4 years to 7 years and 9 months
  • With Further Education: Approximately 7 years and 3 months to 10 years and 9 months
