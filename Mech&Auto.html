MECHANICAL AND AUTOMOTIVE

About Mechanical and Automotive

Overview

  • Mechanical Trade
    • Training Areas:

    This includes training in areas such as machining, welding, fitting, and maintenance of mechanical equipment. Students learn to work with different types of machinery and tools, understand mechanical drawings, and gain skills in assembling and disassembling mechanical components.

    Career Opportunities:

    Graduates can work as machinists, maintenance technicians, mechanical fitters, CNC operators, and in various roles within manufacturing industries.

  • Automotive Trade
    • Training Areas:

    This focuses on the repair and maintenance of vehicles. Students learn about the internal combustion engine, vehicle electronics, fuel systems, transmission systems, and braking systems. The curriculum often includes hands-on training in diagnosing and fixing vehicle issues.

    Career Opportunities:

    Graduates can work as automotive mechanics, service technicians, vehicle inspectors, and in other roles within automotive service centers, dealerships, and repair shops.

Curriculum

  • Basic Mechanical Engineering: Fundamental principles of mechanics, properties of materials, and mechanical engineering concepts.
  • Workshop Practices: Hands-on training with tools and machinery used in both mechanical and automotive fields.
  • Automotive Systems: Detailed study of different automotive systems including engines, electrical systems, transmission, and braking systems.
  • Maintenance and Repair: Techniques for maintaining and repairing mechanical and automotive systems, including troubleshooting and problem-solving skills.

Skills Acquired

  • Technical Skills: Proficiency in using tools and machinery, understanding technical drawings, and performing mechanical and automotive repairs.
  • Problem-Solving Skills: Ability to diagnose issues and find effective solutions in both mechanical and automotive contexts.
  • Practical Experience: Hands-on training that simulates real-world scenarios in workshops and labs.

Employment Sectors

  • Manufacturing Industry: Roles in production, maintenance, and quality control of mechanical components.
  • Automotive Service Centers: Positions as mechanics and service technicians dealing with vehicle repair and maintenance.
  • Machinery Maintenance: Jobs in maintaining and repairing industrial machinery and equipment.
  • Automotive Dealerships: Opportunities in servicing and inspecting vehicles.
